Strategic Framework

Why most leagues fail on social
— and how BPT20 won't

P
The generic clone trap

Every domestic T20 league uses the same template: score graphics, player portraits, match previews in the exact format used by IPL, with a different logo. BPT20's current social feed has no distinct visual identity, no Bengal-specific voice, and no content that wouldn't work for any cricket league in India.

A
40K followers ≠ 40K fans

There's a critical difference between followers and fans. Followers give you a number. Fans give you a movement. Without cultural ownership — without content that makes a fan in Murshidabad say "amader league" — BPT20 is invisible in the off-season and forgettable during it. Passive audiences don't generate sponsor ROI.

S
The Rootanian Belonging Framework

We don't build audiences. We build belonging. When fans feel cultural ownership of a league — when the content feels like it grew up in the same north Kolkata para they did — every social metric changes permanently. Our framework turns BPT20 from a cricket league into Bengal's cricket identity. That's a brand defensible against any competitor.

"Most leagues build audiences. The strongest leagues build belonging. BPT20 doesn't need to adapt cricket for Bengal — it needs to make cricket feel like it was born here."

— Rootanian Strategic POV, BPT20 Season 3

Signature Content IPs

Six formats no one else can copy.

These are intellectual properties — not just content ideas. Repeatable, branded, Bengal-native. Each is a sponsor integration slot and a BPT20 social media asset that compounds in value with every season.

01
IP Series
Addabaazi with Players
Raw conversations. No scripts. Just vibes.

Players in casual settings — tea in hand, no media training, no prepared answers. Pure banter. Rivals expose each other. Para cricket stories come alive. Bengalis understand adda culture better than anyone. This series makes players feel like people your fans have known for years, not just cricketers they watch twice a week.

3–5 min Reels / YouTube · 2–3 eps/week
02
IP Series
30 Second Super Over
30 seconds. Locker room secrets. Infinite shares.

Rapid-fire questions under the clock. "Pressure mein sabse pehle kaun excuse deta hai?" No edits. No retakes. Built for algorithmic velocity — short, punchy, infinitely replayable. This format is designed to go viral within cricket Twitter before Rootanian even has to amplify it.

60–90 sec Reels · Pre-match + mid-tournament
03
IP Series
Adda Premier League
Where cricket meets Bengal's greatest tradition.

Multi-player adda sessions. 3–4 players debate, share stories, and banter across team lines. Best knock of their life? District cricket rivalries? Which franchise has the most loyal fans? Builds cross-team narrative threads that fans follow like a drama series. Natural chai brand integration slot.

8–12 min YouTube + 90s IG cut · Weekly
04
IP Series
Kolkata Check
How well do BPT20 players really know this city?

Rosogolla vs Mishti Doi. Bangla slang tests. Mystery para locations. Every episode positions players as proud locals or lovable outsiders.

5–8 min YouTube · 2× per week
05
IP Series
Dugout Uncut
The real game unfolds where cameras don't go.

Raw match-day access — dugout celebrations, frustration after a dropped catch, strategy sessions. Unfiltered. The broadcast can't do this. Creates the deepest fan loyalty through genuine insider access.

Match-day Stories + 3–4 min YouTube
06
IP Series
Para Ki Team
Where every franchise belongs to a neighbourhood.

District-by-district fan features — which para backs which team, what do local streets look like on match day? Each of the 8 franchise fanbases gets their own spotlight. Drives regional pride, user-generated content loops, and hyperlocal sharing across every district of Bengal. The content format that turns geography into identity.

45–90 sec Instagram Reels · Weekly throughout tournament
